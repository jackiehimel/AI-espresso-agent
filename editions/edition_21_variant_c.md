## Hackers stole 2FA codes from Copilot users through search hijacking

A vulnerability called SearchLeak let attackers inject invisible text into Copilot results that tricked users into leaking their two-factor codes. The exploit worked by poisoning search results with hidden instructions that made Copilot ask users to share sensitive data — then send it to attacker-controlled servers.

*LLMs can be weaponized through their own search features, turning helpful AI into a phishing vector.*

[Ars Technica — AI](https://arstechnica.com/security/2026/06/critical-copilot-vulnerability-allowed-hackers-to-seal-2fa-code-from-users/) · Jun 16
